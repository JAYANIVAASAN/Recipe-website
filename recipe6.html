ice cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 4 ounces (about 120g) bittersweet or semisweet chocolate, chopped
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, sugar, salt, and cocoa powder. Heat the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it reaches a simmer. Remove from heat.
  2. Add the chopped chocolate to the hot cream mixture and stir until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth. Slowly pour about 1/2 cup of the hot chocolate mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to temper the eggs and prevent them from curdling.
  4. Gradually whisk the egg mixture back into the saucepan with the remaining chocolate mixture.
  5. Cook the mixture over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon, about 5-7 minutes. Do not let it boil.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat and strain the custard through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl to remove any cooked egg bits.
  7. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  8. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap, pressing it directly onto the surface of the custard to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the custard in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight until completely cold.
  9. Once the custard is chilled, churn it in an ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions until it reaches a soft-serve consistency, about 20-25 minutes.
  10. Transfer the churned ice cream to an airtight container and freeze for at least 4 hours or until firm.
  11. Serve your homemade chocolate ice cream scooped into bowls or cones, or use it to make ice cream sandwiches, floats, or sundaes.
